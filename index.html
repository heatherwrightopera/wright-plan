8
Capital Project Governance
Framework by Sept · Board adoption Oct
▼

The Mesker Front restoration is teaching us something important: we don't yet have a shared framework for how capital projects are identified, funded, and executed. Idea → donor → figure it out has worked, but it won't scale and it creates risk. We use Mesker to build the framework so every project that follows has a defined process from the start.

Framework developed by September · Board adoption by October (timed to budget planning)

Phase 3 Growth & Continuity Year 2+

Phase 3 priorities will be shaped by what we learn in Phases 1 and 2. We will not pursue growth we are not ready to sustain.

Youth Programming: If 2026 pilots validate youth engagement as natural and sustainable, we develop structured programming with clear outcomes for 2027 and beyond.

Capital Project Execution: With a governance framework in place, future projects (kitchen and beyond) move forward with defined scope, funding plans, and clear leadership roles.

What Success Looks Like in 2026

We will know this year has been a success when:

  • ◎Programming is delivered consistently at a high standard, proving 2025 was not a fluke but a new baseline
  • ◎Our partner listening tour is complete, documented, and already shaping 2027 planning
  • ◎Every affected donor has been personally contacted and relationships are actively being rebuilt
  • ◎Youth engagement pilots are documented with clear learnings captured
  • ◎A financial sustainability roadmap is in place with board-adopted reserve and contingency policies
  • ◎Community perception of The Wright as welcoming and accessible is demonstrably improving
  • ◎The Mesker front restoration is actively advancing with documented milestones
  • ◎Music programming has launched with two well-executed shows
  • ◎Our mission has been updated, voted on, and shared
  • ◎Our program evaluation framework is fully implemented and actively being used
